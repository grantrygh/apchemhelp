Gases

Name Formula
Boyle's Law
As the volume of a gas increases, pressure decreases (vice-versa), as long as mass and temperature remain constant.
Charles' Law
As the temperature of a gas increases, volume increases (vice-versa), as long as mass and pressure remain constant.
Gay-Lussac's Law
As the temperature of a gas increases, pressure increases (vice-versa), as long as mass and volume remain constant.
Combined Gas Law
Rearrangement of Charles' and Boyle's Laws.
Avogadro's Law
As the amount of moles of a gas increases, volume increases (vice-versa), as long as pressure and temperature remain constant.
Ideal Gas Law
n = Number of moles, R = Gas constant
Density/Molar Mass
Graham's Law of Effusion
The lighter gas will diffuse more than the heavier gas.
Root Mean Square Velocity
Urms = Root Mean Square

M = Molar Mass (kilograms)
Dalton's Law
The total pressure of a mixture of gases is equal to the sum of the partial pressures of the component gases.
Mole Fraction
Ratio of moles of gas A to the total number of moles of all components.
Van der Waal's Equation
Corrects the Ideal Gas Law for the volume of, and attractive forces between, gas molecules. a corrects pressure, taking into account molecular interactions. b corrects volume, as molecules in real gases have volume and take up space. (Wiki a and b values)
Standard Pressures
Condition used to compare experimental results from different places.
Standard Temperatures
Condition used to compare experimental results from different places.
